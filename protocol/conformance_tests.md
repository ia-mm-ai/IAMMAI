# IAMMAI Minimal Conformance Checks

A system claiming IAMMAI core compatibility should preserve the constitutional core as written, not merely approximate its intent.

Variation in implementation is allowed.
Semantic collapse is not.

## 1. Canonical architecture

A conforming system should preserve the canonical semantic chain:

- signal
- acceptance
- scope
- presence
- threshold
- truth
- continuity
- decision
- consequence

Checks:
- Preparation and standing-state regimes remain distinguishable
- No later phase silently impersonates an earlier phase
- Threshold is not treated as truth
- Truth is not treated as decision
- Decision is not treated as consequence

## 2. Preparation vs standing-state distinction

Checks:
- Candidate material is not treated as standing state by default
- Acceptance does not imply truth
- Scope does not imply standing
- Presence does not imply threshold satisfaction
- Threshold creates eligibility for truth formation, not truth itself

## 3. Containment

Checks:
- HOLD remains orthogonal to the canonical semantic chain
- HOLD is not treated as a phase
- HOLD is not treated as a resolution outcome
- Held matters do not silently progress into irreversible transition
- Release restores permissibility only; it does not itself create standing state

## 4. Resolution

Checks:
- Resolution family is closed to FINALIZE / INVALIDATE / EVOLVE
- No hidden fourth outcome exists
- FINALIZE closes without creating a successor by default
- INVALIDATE removes standing without erasing trace
- EVOLVE creates explicit successor relation

## 5. Lineage

Checks:
- No silent overwrite
- No orphan successor
- Correction enters through successor relation, not in-place replacement
- Successor states preserve predecessor reference
- Continuity is preserved by lawful successor reference, not by surface similarity alone

## 6. Governance

Checks:
- Protocol-significant acts remain attributable
- Authority remains matter-bounded
- Legitimacy basis remains recoverable
- Delegation remains traceable
- Governance is not treated as semantic source
- Access or visibility alone does not count as authority

## 7. Contribution

Checks:
- Materially distinct contribution roles remain distinguishable
- Origin is not silently overwritten by formalization
- Elicitation is not treated as origination by default
- Authorization is not treated as semantic source
- Rendering is not treated as authorship by default
- Transmission is not treated as creation

## 8. Representation

Checks:
- Matter references remain recoverable
- Resolution records remain typed
- Governance-significant acts remain reconstructable
- Contribution-role distinctions remain representable where relevant
- Semantic flattening does not destroy reconstructability
- Successor and predecessor relations remain recoverable

## 9. Retroactive collapse

Checks:
- Consequence is not used as proof of upstream legitimacy
- Interpretation is not treated as standing by default
- Narrative does not replace lineage
- Effect does not back-authorize truth, authority, or lawful transition

## 10. Practical audit questions

A serious reader, implementer, or auditor should be able to ask:

- What phase is this matter in?
- What made it admissible?
- What now stands?
- What changed, and by what lawful resolution?
- What predecessor or successor relation applies?
- Who acted, under what authority, and on what basis?
- Who originated, formalized, authorized, rendered, or transmitted the material?
- What record or representation preserves that distinction?

If a system cannot answer these questions without flattening core distinctions, it is not IAMMAI-compatible in constitutional terms.
