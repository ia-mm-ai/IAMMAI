# IAMMAI Continuity Emission Rules v0

## 1. Purpose

This file defines the first bounded continuity emission rules for IAMMAI.

Its job is to explain when a continuity turn should be emitted, what kinds of runs or turns qualify, what minimal conditions must be met before a continuity turn can exist, and how continuity emission remains subordinate to canonical preservation.

It does not yet try to define:

- code
- a schema
- a graph model
- a visualization system
- a final local emission mechanism
- a full continuity geometry

It is a bounded architectural document for the first continuity emission rules.

## 2. First Principle

Continuity emission depends on preserved canonical reality.

Continuity turns are emitted only where a bounded run or turn has enough preserved truth to support lawful continuity.  
Continuity must remain append-oriented.  
Continuity must not overwrite, mutate, or reinterpret canonical artifacts.  
Continuity serves the body’s memory of lawful becoming, not narrative convenience.

In practical terms, continuity emission may occur only after the canonical artifacts needed to anchor the turn have already been preserved and remain reconstructable.

## 3. Why Continuity Emission Rules Are Needed

Defining a continuity turn is not yet enough.

The body must also know when a continuity turn can lawfully exist. Without emission rules, continuity can be produced too early, on incomplete preservation, or on derivative appearance alone.

Continuity emission must therefore remain bounded and explicit. That is what prevents premature or cosmetic continuity, and what keeps continuity subordinate to the registry rather than drifting into a sovereign interpretation layer.

## 4. Emission Posture

For v0, continuity emission is run-centric.

One successful bounded run generally corresponds to one continuity turn.

This means the continuity artifact remains a continuity turn, but the emission decision is made at the level of the bounded run whose preserved artifact set can already be judged lawfully and reconstructably.

For v0, partial completion and visible non-passage do not emit continuity turns. They remain canonically preserved and may matter later, but they do not yet become continuity turns.

This is the safest bounded posture because the current body already judges success, partial completion, and non-passage at run level, and because continuity must not be emitted merely because something happened.

## 5. Minimum Conditions for Emitting a Continuity Turn

Before a continuity turn may exist, the following minimum conditions should be satisfied:

- a bounded run identity exists
- a bounded matter relation exists
- preserved canonical artifacts sufficient to anchor the turn exist
- the relation between run, matter, and preserved artifacts is reconstructable
- no silent substitution has been made for missing canonical records

In the current bounded slice, this means the successful run must have the canonical artifacts required for that success path preserved in authoritative form rather than only implied by summary or display.

## 6. Successful Runs, Finalized Truths, and Other Outcomes

Continuity turns should not be restricted only to finalized truths.

Continuity attaches to preserved lawful becoming, not only to final closure.

For v0, the qualifying class is successful lawful bounded runs whose preserved canonical artifacts are sufficient to anchor what actually occurred. In the current embodiment slice, that includes successful lawful truth-forming runs with preserved validation, witness, governance, transition, state, and registry-backed canonical records.

V0 therefore does not require FINALIZE or any later closure state before continuity can be emitted.

Partial completion and visible non-passage may still be canonically important, but they do not yet become continuity turns in v0.

## 7. Success, Partial Completion, and Non-Passage

Continuity emission in v0 should treat the main outcome classes as follows:

- successful lawful runs emit a continuity turn when the minimum conditions are met
- partial completion does not emit a continuity turn in v0
- visible non-passage, denial, hold, failure, or validation non-passage do not emit a continuity turn in v0

This does not diminish the importance of partial completion or non-passage. It keeps them visible in canonical preservation without prematurely asserting continuity where the bounded run did not complete as a successful lawful turn.

## 8. Registry Relation

Continuity depends on canonical preservation in the registry.

Continuity does not replace registry truth.  
Continuity may reference registry-preserved artifacts.  
Failed canonical preservation must block or constrain continuity emission.

If the required canonical artifacts for a qualifying successful run were not actually preserved, continuity must not be emitted as though authoritative continuity already exists.

## 9. Predecessor / Successor Posture

In v0, predecessor turn relation is optional.

It becomes relevant only when more than one successful continuity-bearing turn exists and lawful relation between them can be stated without guesswork.

Continuity must remain append-oriented. A later turn may reference a predecessor turn where one exists, but predecessor or successor relation must not be fabricated prematurely, inferred from naming similarity, or backfilled by convenience.

Successor relation should therefore arise by later appended continuity turns, not by in-place mutation of earlier ones.

## 10. What Continuity Emission Must Not Do

A conforming continuity emission rule set must not allow:

- a continuity turn without sufficient canonical artifacts
- a continuity turn to stand in for missing state, transition, governance, witness, or validation records
- a continuity turn to be emitted merely because a UI, export, or summary exists
- a continuity turn to flatten multiple distinct runs into one vague sequence
- a continuity turn to create meaning beyond preserved artifacts
- a continuity turn to be treated as the whole system memory instead of a bounded continuity layer above the registry

## 11. Relation to Future Continuity Work

After the emission rules are clear, later steps may include:

- a continuity-turn schema
- local continuity artifact emission
- predecessor or successor linkage across runs
- richer continuity geometry later, if lawfully justified

Those are later derivation questions. They should not be smuggled into v0 emission rules.

## 12. Summary

This document defines the first bounded continuity emission rules of IAMMAI as the conditions under which a continuity turn may be emitted above preserved canonical reality without replacing it.

Its purpose is to keep continuity small, append-oriented, and lawfully anchored by requiring successful bounded runs with sufficient registry-preserved artifacts, while leaving partial completion and visible non-passage canonically preserved but not yet continuity-bearing in v0.

**One-Line Definition:** `CONTINUITY_EMISSION_RULES_v0.md` defines the first bounded continuity emission rules of IAMMAI as the run-centric v0 rule that emits one continuity turn for one successful lawful bounded run whose preserved canonical artifacts are sufficient to anchor lawful becoming without replacing registry truth.
